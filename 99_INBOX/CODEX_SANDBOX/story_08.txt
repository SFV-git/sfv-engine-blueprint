The survey ship woke Mara before the alarm, whispering that a new moon had appeared beside Saturn. No telescope had seen it arrive; one moment there was empty space, and the next there was a silver sphere reflecting stars that did not belong to the Solar System.

Mara flew close enough to watch its surface unfold into a vast mechanical iris. A signal entered her ship, not as sound or light, but as a memory of Earth beneath unfamiliar constellations. The moon was a vessel, and it had been searching for humanity since before humanity existed.

She transmitted a single question: Why? The answer filled every screen with an image of the Sun growing dim, followed by coordinates beyond the edge of known space. Mara turned her ship homeward, carrying both a warning and an invitation.
