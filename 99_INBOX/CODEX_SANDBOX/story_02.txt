At the edge of Saturn's rings, the survey ship Meridian discovered a moon that appeared on no chart. Its surface shimmered like dark glass, reflecting constellations that had vanished millions of years ago.

Captain Imani descended alone and found a small machine humming beneath the ice. When she touched it, the stars overhead rearranged themselves, and the Meridian's clocks began counting backward.

She understood the machine was not showing the past; it was rebuilding it. Imani shut it down one second before her ship disappeared, then carried home a single warm crystal that whispered tomorrow's coordinates.
