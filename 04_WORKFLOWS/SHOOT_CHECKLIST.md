# SHOOT_CHECKLIST.md

---

## 📌 **STUDIO (50+ Models)**  
*A comprehensive checklist for large-scale studio shoots with multiple models, teams, and complex setups.*

---

### **1. Pre-Shoot Planning**  
- [ ] Confirm **model call times**, travel logistics, and accommodation (if applicable).  
- [ ] Finalize **cast list** (names, contact info, model releases, and contracts).  
- [ ] Coordinate with **stylists, makeup artists, and hairdressers** for wardrobe and look breakdowns.  
- [ ] Schedule **tech scouts** to inspect studio space, lighting, and equipment.  
- [ ] Plan **shoot timeline** (e.g., 8:00 AM – 12:00 PM for first block, 1:00 PM – 5:00 PM for second block).  
- [ ] Prepare **model call sheets** with shoot details, wardrobe, and timing.  
- [ ] Confirm **props, backdrops, and set designs** with art directors.  
- [ ] Ensure **equipment** (cameras, lenses, lighting, drones) is ordered and tested.  
- [ ] Assign **team roles** (lead photographer, assistant, model coordinator, etc.).  

---

### **2. Model Management**  
- [ ] Conduct **model check-in** (15–30 minutes before shoot start) for:  
  - ✅ ID verification and model releases.  
  - ✅ Wardrobe fitting and adjustments.  
  - ✅ Hair/makeup touch-ups.  
- [ ] Prepare **model call times** and ensure punctuality (use a centralized app or system).  
- [ ] Assign **model groups** (e.g., Group A: 1–10, Group B: 11–20) for efficient coordination.  
- [ ] Provide **model amenities** (water, snacks, rest areas).  
- [ ] Schedule **model breaks** (every 2 hours) to avoid fatigue.  

---

### **3. Wardrobe & Makeup**  
- [ ] Confirm **wardrobe inventory** (clothing, accessories, shoes) and pre-shoot fittings.  
- [ ] Label all wardrobe items with model names and shoot-specific tags.  
- [ ] Prepare **emergency wardrobe kits** (extra socks, undergarments, etc.).  
- [ ] Ensure **makeup artists** have access to all required products (e.g., false lashes, prosthetics).  
- [ ] Test **wardrobe durability** (e.g., fabric resistance to lighting, movement).  

---

### **4. Lighting & Tech Setup**  
- [ ] Conduct **lighting tech check** (softboxes, strobes, modifiers).  
- [ ] Test **camera settings** (ISO, aperture, shutter speed) for studio conditions.  
- [ ] Ensure **backup equipment** (chargers, memory cards, extra lights) is on-site.  
- [ ] Set up **drone or aerial photography** if required (check airspace permits).  
- [ ] Confirm **sound equipment** (microphones, mixers) for video or voiceover shoots.  

---

### **5. During the Shoot**  
- [ ] Monitor **model morale** and address concerns immediately.  
- [ ] Track **shoot progress** against the timeline (use a shared digital board).  
- [ ] Ensure **consistent lighting** across all model groups.  
- [ ] Capture **test shots** for each model to adjust lighting/posing.  
- [ ] Coordinate **model transitions** between blocks (e.g., 12:00 PM break).  

---

### **6. Post-Shoot Wrap-Up**  
- [ ] Conduct **model sign-off** (confirm payment, thank them, provide contact info).  
- [ ] Secure **wardrobe and props** for storage or return.  
- [ ] Backup **digital files** to cloud or external drives.  
- [ ] Send **thank-you notes** to models, team, and vendors.  
- [ ] Schedule **retouching and editing** with post-production team.  

---

## 🎉 **EVENTS (On-Site Setup)**  
*A checklist for event photography, including weddings, corporate functions, and live events.*

---

### **1. Pre-Event Preparation**  
- [ ] Scout **event location** (check for lighting, power sources, and space constraints).  
- [ ] Confirm **permits** (e.g., photography licenses, drone approvals).  
- [ ] Coordinate with **event organizers** for access, timing, and restrictions.  
- [ ] Prepare **equipment list** (cameras, lenses, tripods, flashes, batteries).  
- [ ] Arrange **transportation** for team and gear (if off-site).  
- [ ] Plan **backup plans** for weather, power outages, or venue changes.  

---

### **2. On-Site Setup**  
- [ ] Arrive **1–2 hours early** to set up and test equipment.  
- [ ] Secure **power sources** and check for stable internet (for live streaming).  
- [ ] Set up **tripods, monopods, and camera rigs** in key areas (e.g., entrance, stage).  
- [ ] Test **lighting** (natural or artificial) and adjust for event mood.  
- [ ] Confirm **access to restricted areas** (e.g., VIP zones, backstage).  

---

### **3. During the Event**  
- [ ] Capture **key moments** (e.g., speeches, dances, guest arrivals).  
- [ ] Monitor **crowd movement** to avoid blocking shots.  
- [ ] Use **discreet shooting techniques** (e.g., long lenses for candid shots).  
- [ ] Coordinate with **event staff** for access to special moments (e.g., cake cutting).  
- [ ] Ensure **team communication** via headsets or shared apps.  

---

### **4. Post-Event Wrap-Up**  
- [ ] Secure **equipment** and return to storage or transport.  
- [ ] Backup **photos/videos** to cloud or external drives.  
- [ ] Send **event summary** to organizers (e.g., highlights, missing shots).  
- [ ] Collect **feedback** from clients and event team.  
- [ ] Invoice and follow up on **payment** with organizers.  

---

## 🏃‍♂️ **ATHLETICS**  
*A checklist for sports, fitness, and athletic-themed shoots (e.g., athletes, workouts, competitions).*

---

### **1. Pre-Shoot Preparation**  
- [ ] Coordinate with **athletes** for availability, warm-up times, and shoot goals.  
- [ ] Confirm **sports-specific equipment** (e.g., gear, uniforms, safety wear).  
- [ ] Scout **location** (e.g., gym, track, outdoor field) and check for safety hazards.  
- [ ] Arrange **transportation** for athletes (if needed).  
- [ ] Plan **shoot timeline** around training sessions or competitions.  
- [ ] Ensure **safety protocols** (e.g., first aid kits, hydration stations).  

---

### **2. On-Site Setup**  
- [ ] Set up **lighting** for dynamic motion shots (e.g., fast shutter speeds, strobes).  
- [ ] Position **cameras** at strategic angles (e.g., low-angle for power shots).  
- [ ] Use **telephoto lenses** to capture action from a distance.  
- [ ] Confirm **athletic warm-up** and cooldown periods (avoid overexertion).  
- [ ] Ensure **athlete comfort** (e.g., shaded areas, water access).  

---

### **3. During the Shoot**  
- [ ] Capture **key athletic moments** (e.g., jumps, sprints, lifts).  
- [ ] Use **burst mode** for motion blur and sharp focus.  
- [ ] Coordinate with **coaches or trainers** for access to specific drills.  
- [ ] Monitor **athlete performance** and adjust timing as needed.  
- [ ] Use **drone footage** for wide-angle shots (if permitted).  

---

### **4. Post-Shoot Wrap-Up**  
- [ ] Secure **equipment** and return to storage or transport.  
- [ ] Backup **photos/videos** to cloud or external drives.  
- [ ] Send **highlight reels** to athletes and sponsors.  
- [ ] Collect **feedback** from athletes and coaches.  
- [ ] Invoice and follow up on **payment** with clients or organizations.  

---

## 📌 **General Notes**  
- Always **double-check permits** for studio, events, and athletics (e.g., location, drone, safety).  
- Maintain **emergency contact lists** for each branch (e.g., medical, police, venue staff).  
- Use **digital tools** (e.g., Trello, Google Sheets) for real-time coordination.  
- Prioritize **model/athlete safety** and comfort in all shoots.  

---  
**END OF CHECKLIST**
