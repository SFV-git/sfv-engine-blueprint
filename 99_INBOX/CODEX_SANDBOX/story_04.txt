At dawn, the research vessel Meridian woke beneath the ice of Europa. Its crew had slept for seven years, but the ship's sensors insisted that something below the frozen ocean had been waiting much longer.

Commander Vale lowered a probe into the black water. Instead of pressure readings, it returned a sequence of prime numbers, followed by a map of Earth drawn from a viewpoint far beyond the solar system.

The crew prepared to transmit the discovery home, but the ice above them began to sing in the same numerical rhythm. Vale stopped the message and listened as the final pattern translated itself: We remember sending you.
